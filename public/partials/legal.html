Hold up, is all this legal?

I’m glad you asked - yes it is. The primary reason restaurants don’t donate their leftover food is liability: if the food brings someone harm, it’d be the restaurant’s fault, right? Not exactly.

See, food waste has been a concern in America for quite some time, and the liability restaurants face has always hindered progress in this front. Luckily though, a cool guy named Bill Clinton (the former president, in case you were wondering), passed a bill called the Bill Emerson good samaritan food donation act of 1996. The Bill bill passed by Bill (hehe) allows restaurants and businesses to donate their leftover food with no liabilty - and yes, it was passed a whole 20 years ago.
